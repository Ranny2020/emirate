TIKAU EMIRATE

THE RULERS OF TIKAU



NDIIRAMA JIJI (1750 - 1758)

Ndiirama Jiiji who reigned between 1750 and 1758 CE took over the mantle of leadership; amd he was reported to have completed the building of Tikau fortification walls. He was succeed by "Ndiirama Matata".




NDIIRAMA MATAATA (1758 - 1842)

Ndiirama Mataata who reigned from 1758 to 1842 CE, he was reported to have been confronted and defeated by the Kanem-Borno warriors. Matata was also succeeded by "Mai Warkati".




MAI (DEDE) WARKATI (1842 - 1862)

Mai Warkati who reigned between 1842 t0 1852 CE, he was reported to be first Tikau ruler to adopt the Kanuri title "Mai" wih the death of Mai Warkati, Mai Usaku took over the throne.




MAI USAKU (1862 - 1887)

Mai Usaku who reigned from 1862 to 1887 CE came to power. He was succeeded by the famous Tikau ruler "Mai Saaban".




MAI SAABAN (1887 - 1921)

Mai Saaban, who ruled from 1887 to 1921 CE. it was during his reign that Tikau dynasty attained it greatness and experienced mass territorial expansion which was only halted by the arrival of the British Colonial forces at the dawn of the 20th century. Likewise, it was also during his era that British Colonialist Conquered the area; and he was reported to have resisted the colonial conquest and subjugation of his authority to Fika rulership despite the power of the Colonialists. Therefore Saaban's Era can be regarded as a turning point in the history of Tikau Emirate. After the death of Mai Saaban, "Mai Gaski Usaku" succeeded him.

MAI GASKI USAKU (1921 - 1924)

Mai Gaski Usaku, who reigned from 1921 to 1924 CE. He was succeeded by Mai Saaban's Son, "Mai Grema".




MAI GREMA (1924 - 1945)

Mai Grema took the power and reigned between 1924 to 1945 CE. It was during his reign that the British Colonialist finally relocated the Fika Emirate's administrative headquarters from Fika Village to Potiskum town, and included Tikau in the new Fika Emirate. So too were other chieftaincies within the defunct Karai - Karai commonwealth. While relating the imposition of Fika Emirate's rulership over Tikau dynasty by British Colonial Masters, Sule 2014 asserted that the later had consequently caused a lot of set back on Tikau dynasty. It was the period during which the area was relegated to the status of a village area, thereby lost kts sovereignty or entity. In consequence, Mai Grema became the first village head of Tikau village area with the headquarters at Tikau. As a good leader, he was able to mobilize his people to accomodate and endure the retrogressive development in the status of their domain. in 1945, Mai Grema died, and was succeeded by his son; "Mai Abubakar Shuwa Ibn Grema".

ALHAJI ABUBAKAR SHUWA IBN GREMA (1945 - 2001)

Alhaji Abubakar Shuwa Ibn Grema was the first Western Educated Chief. He was elevated to the status of a district head in 1962 as a result of the creation of three districts areas under the Fika Native Authority. The districts were Fika, Nangere, and Potiskum.His appointment as a district head partially restored shikau's pre-colonial portion of land, separarted by the British. Alhaji Abubakar Shuwa Ibn Grema was the 10th ruler of Tikau, later he was appointed District of Nngere. He was elevated to the status of Emir of Tikau Emirate in 1993 thereby becoming the first Emir of Tikau with second class staff of office, However, this was short lived as the Emirate was soon dissolved by the Military Administrator of the state, CP Dabo Aliyu , when the Military regime of Late Gen. Sani Abacha took over later that year. However , with the return of Alhaji Bukar Abba Ibrahim as the Executive Governor of Yobe State for the second time in 1999, the dissolved emirates were reinstated. Alhaji Abubakar Shuwa Ibn Grema was reappointed as the Mai of Tikau in 2001. He served in that capacity for almost three years and died on 17th July, 2001. After the death, his son "Alhaji Muhammadu Abubakar Ibn Grema" succeeded him.

ALHAJI MUHAMMADU ABUBAKAR IBN GREMA (2001 - 2024)

Alh. Dr. Muhammadu Abubakar Ibn Grema Was the 11th ruler of Tikau succeeded him as Mai of Tikau on 21st July, 2001. He was turbaned on saturday 11th may 2002. He was uppgraded to first class Emir on 28th may,2007 by Alhaji Bukar Abba Ibrahim, FNIQS, the Ex-Governor of Yobe State. Similarly, on 5th August 2010, Alhaji Muhammadu Abubakar Ibn Grema was appointed the first Pioneer Chancellor of Yobe state University, Damaturu, by Alhaji Ibrahim Gaidam, FCNA, FCPA, Ex-Executive Governor of Yobe state.

ALHAJI ABUBAKAR MUHAMMADU IBN GREMA (2024 - TILL DATE)
THE PRESENT EMIR OF TIKAU EMIRATE

The eldest of Alh. Dr. Muhammadu Abubakar Ibn Grema, the late Emir of Tikau. The 12th ruler of Tikau Emirate. Alhaji Abubakar Muhammadu Ibn Grema has a Bachelor of Agric Economics & Extension, from Bayero University Kano,an MSc in Agric Economics from University of Readings, United Kingdom(Applied Econometrics, Development and Resource Economics) and a PhD from Yobe State University (inview) in Rural Development. He has worked in various capacities at Yobe state University. Before his appointment, Abubakar Muhammadu Ibn Gream, was the Deputy Director Agric services, at Yobe State University and also was the district head of Sabon Gari and Yerima of Tikau Emirate.
